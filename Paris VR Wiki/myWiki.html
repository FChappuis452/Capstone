Paris VR Project

The Paris VR 2.0 project was commissioned by Brant County and Professor Russell Foubert.

This project aims to utilize Virtual Reality technology to allow people to interact with the rich history of Paris, Ontario.

The Paris VR 2.0 solution consists of 2 applications; a toolkit to create VR tourism apps, and a VR tourism app (made with the toolkit) that offers a tour through the history of Paris' mill-races.

This project, created by The Weeping Angels (no Dr. affiliations), acts as a Capstone project for the Software Engineering Technology program at Conestoga College

VR Tourism Toolkit Application

The VR Tourism Toolkit allows for the creation and customization of VR Tourism Applications

Offering everything from map configuration to interactible-data configuration, the toolkit will allow for the easy creation of a VR app for any city.

Paris VR 2.0 Application

The Paris VR 2.0 application offers a Virtual-Reality tourism experience through the rich history of Paris, Ontario

Viewing the history of Paris in an interactive environment will help people learn about the mill-races that used to be used to transport materials between the mills in Paris.

By positioning a historical map overtop a current map, Paris VR 2.0 allows for people to switch between the different terrains as they go, seeing what once was and what is now.
